# SAE BAJA — Off-Road Vehicle Design & Manufacturing

## Overview
This repository documents my complete engineering contribution to the SAE BAJA 
competition — an international collegiate design competition where student teams 
design, build, and race a single-seat off-road vehicle against universities 
worldwide. The vehicle must withstand extreme terrain conditions while meeting 
strict SAE rulebook requirements for safety and performance.

I was a core team member responsible for multiple vehicle systems — from initial 
CAD design and structural simulation through to physical fabrication and 
competition readiness.

---

## My Responsibilities

### Design & CAD (SolidWorks / Creo / AutoCAD)
- Developed 3D models of vehicle sub-systems including chassis, drivetrain 
  mounts, and suspension geometry
- Created detailed 2D engineering drawings for manufacturing using AutoCAD
- Ensured all components complied with SAE BAJA rulebook dimensional 
  and safety requirements
- Performed design iterations based on simulation feedback and weight targets

### Structural Analysis (ANSYS FEA)
- Conducted Finite Element Analysis (FEA) on critical structural components 
  including roll cage joints and suspension members
- Analysed stress distribution, deformation, and factor of safety under 
  simulated off-road load cases
- Used simulation results to optimise geometry and reduce weight without 
  compromising structural integrity

### Drivetrain & Powertrain
- Worked on drivetrain layout including CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission) 
  integration with the Briggs & Stratton engine
- Analysed torque transfer, gear reduction requirements, and power delivery 
  to the rear axle
- Contributed to selection and sizing of drivetrain components based on 
  performance and weight constraints

### Suspension & Steering
- Designed and analysed the suspension geometry for off-road performance 
  (bump travel, camber change, and roll stiffness)
- Worked on A-arm / double wishbone suspension configuration
- Contributed to steering geometry setup including Ackermann angle and 
  turning radius calculations
- Validated suspension kinematics through SolidWorks motion analysis

### Manufacturing & Fabrication
- Actively involved in physical fabrication of the vehicle frame and 
  sub-assemblies
- Worked with processes including MIG welding, metal cutting, drilling, 
  and assembly fitting
- Translated CAD designs into real manufactured components, ensuring 
  dimensional accuracy and structural quality
- Participated in vehicle assembly, fitment checks, and pre-competition 
  inspection
